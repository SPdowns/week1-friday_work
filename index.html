    2. I started recording music in 2003, and started recording others professionally in 2005. I had a professinal grade studio; a live sound package for small groups; and basic musician skills. After two years of barley making money, I switched career paths.

  2. Sketch Comedy Writer

After almost four years of not moving fast enough, I had to return to San Diego.

  3. Retail

    4. After all else failed, retail is where I fell down too. Enjoying people, I thought I could feel content with making a living in that kind of profession. Unfortunetly, employees don't make a livable wage, customers aren't considered people, and being treated subhumanly finally took its toll.

So, I'm excited about what these new coding skills will bring. But more excited about learning to work with others. And especially excited about Resume and Cover letter training and the mock interviews. If my confidence rises with applying for work, then I know I will have a life that isn't plauged by failing to earn a wage that can support me and a family.
